How to add text to photos on Photoshop

Assembly time: 30 minutes

Do you want to learn how to make a cool image with text on it using photoshop? Follow these steps to make a fun social media post!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Computer or laptop
  • Adobe photoshop
  • Text to put on image (a quote, instructions, facts, etc.)
  • Photo, either taken by yourself or borrowed from the internet

  1. Find a photo to use and save it.

    If you have a photo you took yourself you want to use, you can definately use that! If not, you can find photos online using Flickr. Make sure if you find a photo you like, you use the correct creative commons license qualifications and give the author of the photo credit if needed.

    Photos you can find on Flickr

  2. Create a new document.

    You can open your photo by clicking on "Create new" on the left-hand side of Photoshop's homepage, picking the size your want, and creating a blank document.

  3. Open your photo

    You can drag your photo from your downloads into photoshop with your mouse, and it will open a new document of your photo. Make sure the photo is an unlocked layer, and then you can copy and paste it into the first blank document (so you have a blank background, or you can change the color if you wanted a different color background) If it does not let you open your photo, you may to save it as a different file, such as JPEG or PNG.

  4. Style your photo.

    The best way to style a photo with text going on it would be to lower the opacity. You can do that by clicking the layer the image is on, making sure it is unlocked, and making the opacity lower. The opacity will be on the righthand side above the layers.

  5. Find the right text.

    Decide what text you want for your photo! This can be a quote, instructions, a warning, or many different things, as long as it fits with the photo!

  6. Style your text.

    Make sure your text is legible and creative! Find the right font to use and make sure it looks good against the photo.

  7. Save your photo.

    Save your new photo! Make sure to save it twice: one as a JPEG or PNG to publish, and the other as a PSD, in order to go back and edit it if you ever want to change anything later on.
